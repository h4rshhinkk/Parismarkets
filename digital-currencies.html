Litecoin (LTC)

  • Bitcoin Cash was created as a result of counteracting a prolonged Bitcoin scalability problem

An Example of
Leveraged CFD Trading

Suppose you want to trade CFDs, where the underlying asset is the XAUUSD a Metal, also known as Gold vs US Dollar. Let us suppose that the XAUUSD is trading at:

Now, in the next hour, if the price moves to 1793/1793.1, you have a winning trade. You could close your position by selling at the current price of 1793 USD.

You decide to buy 100 oz of XAUUSD (1Lot of XAUUSD) because you think that the XAUUSD price will rise in the future. Your margin rate is 0.2% (Account Leverage 500:1) . This means that you need to deposit 0.2% of the total position value into your margin account.

In this case, the price of gold moved in your favor. But, had the price declined instead, moving against your prediction, you could have made a loss. This continuous evaluation of price movements and resultant profit/loss happens daily. Accordingly, it leads to a net return (positive/negative) on your initial margin. In the loss scenario where your free equity, (account balance+ Profit/Loss) falls below the margin requirements (358.4), the broker will issue a margin call. If you fail to deposit the money, and the market moves further against you, when your free equity reaches the 50% of your initial margin the contract will be closed at the current market price, known as “stop out.”

Cryptocurrency Trading - FAQ

What are Cryptocurrency CFDs?

Cryptocurrency CFDs are a new way to trade this revolutionary financial instrument. Just like Forex, you can speculate on the price of major Cryptocurrencies by purchasing a Contract for Difference. Paris Markets offers Cryptocurrency CFD trading in Bitcoin, Litecoin, Ripple ,Ethereum and Bitcoin Cash. Read more about Cryptocurrency CFDs.

How do Cryptocurrency CFDs work?

They work using the same concept as other Contracts for Difference (CFDs). You are trading on the difference in price of a Cryptocurrency from the time a trade is opened and closed. Cryptocurrency CFDs allow you to predict the future change in value of a nominated Cryptocurrency. With interest constantly growing in this emerging asset class, increased levels of liquidity are resulting in lower trading costs. Learn How to Buy and Sell Cryptocurrency using CFDs.

What are the benefits of Crypto CFDs?

Trading Crypto CFDs offers several benefits. Unlike trading fiat currencies, you do not own the underlying asset. The benefit of this is that you do not need a Crypto wallet, removing the need to store the asset. In addition, you can trade in rising and falling prices. This creates more trading opportunities in an asset which is considered high risk and volatile.
